New York – often called New York City or the City of New York to distinguish it from the State of New York, of which it is a part – is the most populous city in the United States and the center of the New York metropolitan area, the premier gateway for legal immigration to the United States and one of the most populous urban agglomerations in the world. A global power city, New York exerts a significant impact upon commerce, finance, media, art,fashion, research, technology, education, and entertainment.

Home to the headquarters of the United Nations, New York is an important center for international diplomacy and has been described as the cultural and financial capital of the world.
TIMES SQUARE
Known colloquially as "The Crossroads of the World" and "The Great White Way," Times Square NYC is one of the most popular commercial intersections in the world! Located in central Manhattan at the junction of Broadway and Seventh Avenue.
LIBERTY ISLAND
The Statue of Liberty, one of New York’s most popular tourist attractions, has captivated travelers from every corner of the world for over one hundred years. One of the most recognizable and well-known women in the world, the Statue of Liberty has a resume that would make any movie star jealous!
WALL STREET
Wall Street New York is located in the famous Financial District neighborhood in Manhattan. While it refers to the neighborhood that includes the New York Stock Exchange, Wall Street New York is also a street name in the lower Manhattan area.
BROOKLYN BRIDGE
Completed in 1883, The Brooklyn Bridge has been one of the most iconic attractions in NYC ever since. It connects the boroughs of Manhattan and Brooklyn over the East River water. Discover all of the best ways to experience this Big Apple Landmark.
